About

A practical guide for people building a real service business

Sierra Services is led by Santiago Sierra in Zurich. I help founders turn an idea or a skill into something more concrete online: a clearer website, a cleaner way to collect enquiries, and the first business systems that stop everything from depending on memory.

I hold a Master's degree in Business Administration from Hochschule Luzern, but the way I work is practical before anything else. I care about making the business understandable, usable, and easier to run.

A lot of founders have the skill, the ambition, and the willingness to work hard, but not yet the business structure around them. The website may not exist, the enquiry path may be messy, the business email is not set up, or the first CRM logic is still missing.

That is the kind of early-stage gap I like helping with. The work can include the website, Google presence, booking and forms, Stripe or invoicing setup, and the first simple systems that make the business feel like more than an idea.

What people tend to value

The work is meant to feel responsible, clear, and useful

I have not filled this site with polished testimonial quotes. What I can say honestly is that certain themes keep coming back in how clients describe the work.

Clear paths, not vague ideas

People value knowing what to do next instead of being left with abstract recommendations.

Good communication

The work is explained in a way clients can follow without needing to decode jargon.

Responsibility

Tasks are handled seriously, with follow-through, rather than as disconnected gigs.

Help across moving parts

It helps when the website, forms, booking, payments, and systems all need to work together.

How I work

Direct, practical, and not overbuilt

  • direct collaboration with the person doing the work
  • recommendations grounded in the stage your business is actually in
  • implementation alongside guidance, not strategy alone
  • systems that should stay usable after the project ends

Where this can go later

Small systems first, then stronger structure when it is deserved

I am also building Sierra Invoices as a Sierra Services product for businesses that mostly need billing. And when a business grows enough that deeper CRM structure becomes useful, HubSpot can become part of the next stage rather than the first thing I push.
