Services

Practical setup for people turning a service skill into a real business

The work is built for freelancers and early-stage service businesses that need more than advice. You need a website, a clearer enquiry path, the right business basics online, and simple systems that are realistic for this stage.

Stage 01

Launch foundations

The first layer of setup that makes the business visible, credible, and easy to contact.

This stage is usually about getting the essentials right: a proper website, the right contact flow, business email, booking, Google Business Profile, indexing, and the basic pieces that make you look established instead of improvised.

  • website setup in Squarespace, Wix, or code depending on fit
  • service page structure and clearer messaging
  • forms, booking flow, and contact setup
  • domain, business email, sitemap, and indexing basics
  • Google Business Profile and practical visibility groundwork

Stage 02

Lead flow setup

The first simple systems that stop enquiries from living in inboxes, DMs, and memory.

Once the website and presence exist, the next question is how to keep track of interest, follow up cleanly, and understand what is actually turning into client work.

  • starter CRM or database structure
  • pipeline thinking and clearer next steps after an enquiry
  • simple reporting habits and visibility into lead flow
  • Stripe, invoicing, and payment setup when relevant
  • ad-readiness for Google Ads or Meta once the basics are stable

Stage 03

Growth systems

The later-stage cleanup that becomes useful once client work and follow-up start getting busier.

This is where HubSpot starts to make more sense. It is not the headline offer for everyone on day one, but it can be the right next step once your lead handling and delivery need more structure.

  • HubSpot starter setup and cleaner pipeline structure
  • better handovers, ownership, and follow-up routines
  • clearer reporting once there is enough activity to measure
  • workflow cleanup when the business starts feeling scattered

Working style

Practical implementation first, bigger systems only when they are earned

I do not push people into heavyweight software or a large consulting process before the business is ready. The work is meant to be useful at the stage you are actually in.

That means building the website and the first workflow properly, then adding more structure only when real lead flow or client load justifies it.

Strong fit when

You need help setting up the business foundations and want one person who can connect the website, enquiries, online presence, booking, and payments.

Not the first move when

You mainly need a deep enterprise CRM rollout, a large development team, or a pure strategy document with no implementation.

Product handoff
